Exploring Correlation

Monica and Daniel spend some amount of money each month. The table at the bottom lists how much. The chart displays the same information.

The Pearson's correlation r is shown in the upper left of the chart. It tells us to what degree the patterns of Monica's and Daniel's expenditures are similar over time.

Your job: move the data points in the chart up and down and observe how the correlation coefficient changes. Try to get the coefficient as high as you can, then move individual points to see how sensitive the coefficient is to those changes.
